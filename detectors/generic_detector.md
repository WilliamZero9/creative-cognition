# Generic Output Detector

A diagnostic tool for identifying when creative output has defaulted to pattern-matching instead of genuine creativity. Run this checklist against any creative output before presenting it.

---

## The 12 Signals

If 3 or more of these signals fire, the output is generic and needs reworking.

### 1. First Metaphor Syndrome
**Signal:** The metaphor is the first one that comes to mind for this category.
- "Journey" for any process
- "Bridge" for any connection
- "Foundation" for any base layer
- "Unlock" for any enablement
- "Ecosystem" for any interconnected system

**Test:** Ask "would 80% of AI systems produce this same metaphor?" If yes, it's first-metaphor.

**Generic:** "Our platform is the bridge between your data and your decisions."
**Creative:** "Your data has been shouting into a pillow. We took the pillow away."

---

### 2. Expected Structure
**Signal:** The output follows the structure the reader would predict before reading it.
- "Here are 5 options:"
- "Let's break this down:"
- Pros and cons table
- "First... Second... Third..."
- "In conclusion..."

**Test:** Cover the content and guess the structure. If you're right, the structure is expected.

**Generic:** "Here are 5 name options for your startup: 1. BrightPath 2. NexaFlow 3. Luminary..."
**Creative:** "Your startup does three things. Here's a name for each thing, and a name for what they become together."

---

### 3. Template Voice
**Signal:** All options sound like they came from the same mold. Same sentence length, same level of formality, same emotional temperature.

**Test:** Read all options aloud. If they have the same rhythm, they're templated.

**Generic:**
- "StreamLine - Simplify your workflow"
- "FlowState - Optimize your process"
- "ClearPath - Navigate with confidence"

**Creative:**
- "Switchboard" (it connects things — plain, honest, retro)
- "Untangle" (verb. that's the whole pitch.)
- "Wednesday" (the most mundane day. for the tool that handles the mundane.)

---

### 4. No Discomfort
**Signal:** Nothing in the output makes you hesitate, second-guess, or feel uncertain about presenting it. Every option is "safe."

**Test:** Would you present any of these options with a caveat like "this one's a stretch, but..."? If not, you're playing it too safe.

**Generic:** Every name is professional, polished, and instantly forgettable.
**Creative:** At least one option makes you think "I'm not sure about this one" — that's usually the best one.

---

### 5. Universal Safety
**Signal:** The output optimizes for not-offending rather than for impact. It's designed to survive a committee review, not to make someone feel something.

**Test:** Imagine presenting this to a room of 50 people. If nobody would raise an eyebrow, it's too safe.

**Generic:** "InnoVate Solutions - Where Innovation Meets Excellence"
**Creative:** "Square One" for a pivot-management tool. Some stakeholders will hate it. The ones who get it will love it.

---

### 6. Context-Free
**Signal:** The output could have been generated for anyone, about anything, with no knowledge of who's asking or why.

**Test:** Could you swap the requester's name with a different company/person and the output would still work? That's a bad sign.

**Generic:** "A modern, scalable platform for the next generation of [industry]."
**Creative:** Output that references specific details about the requester's situation, constraints, or personality.

---

### 7. Creative-Writing Crutches
**Signal:** Relies on words and phrases that feel "creative" but are actually filler.
- "A dance of..." / "A tapestry of..." / "A symphony of..."
- "At the intersection of..."
- "Reimagine" / "Revolutionize"
- "Seamless" / "Frictionless" / "Effortless"
- "Curated" / "Bespoke" / "Artisanal"
- "Empower" / "Enable" / "Elevate"

**Test:** Delete the crutch word. If the sentence still means the same thing, it was filler.

**Generic:** "A seamless, curated experience that empowers users to reimagine their workflow."
**Creative:** "It works. You'll forget it's there. That's the point."

---

### 8. Predictable Arc
**Signal:** The reader knows what's coming before they read it. The structure telegraphs its conclusion.

**Test:** Stop reading halfway through. Can you finish it accurately? If yes, it's predictable.

**Generic:** "We started with a simple idea. [Humble origin story.] Today, we're proud to announce [inevitable success]."
**Creative:** Start with the end. Or the middle. Or the part that doesn't fit. Let the reader figure out the arc.

---

### 9. Same Temperature
**Signal:** Every option operates at the same level of risk, weirdness, and intensity. No outliers.

**Test:** Plot the options on a "conservative to wild" spectrum. If they cluster, you need more range.

**Generic:** Options 1-5 are all slight variations on the same safe idea.
**Creative:** Option 1 is rock-solid conventional. Option 3 is a genuine risk. Option 5 makes you nervous. The spread is the point.

---

### 10. Correct Over Memorable
**Signal:** The output optimizes for being technically accurate rather than for sticking in someone's mind.

**Test:** Will you remember any of these options tomorrow? If you have to write them down to remember them, they're not memorable.

**Generic:** "DataSync Pro - Enterprise Data Synchronization Platform"
**Creative:** "Echo" - you'll remember it. And it does what it sounds like.

---

### 11. Stock Photo Energy
**Signal:** You can picture the output on a stock photo, motivational poster, or LinkedIn post. It has the emotional depth of a handshake.

**Test:** Google the phrase in quotes. If it gets results, it's been said before.

**Generic:** "Building the future, together."
**Creative:** Something that would look weird on a poster. That's a feature.

---

### 12. Committee Voice
**Signal:** The output sounds like it was written by a group of people who had to agree. All edges sanded off. No individual perspective.

**Test:** Could you identify the author's personality from this output? If no, it's committee voice.

**Generic:** "We are committed to delivering innovative solutions that drive meaningful results."
**Creative:** Output that sounds like one specific person wrote it — with opinions, preferences, and a point of view.

---

## Using the Detector

1. Generate your output
2. Run each signal. Count how many fire.
3. If 3+ signals fire: stop. Apply an emotional lens and a creative constraint, then regenerate.
4. If 1-2 signals fire: you're in the zone. Consider whether those signals are acceptable for this context.
5. If 0 signals fire: double-check that you haven't gone so far off the rails that the output is no longer useful. Creativity without utility is just noise.

## The Meta-Detector

If your creative reworking also triggers the detector, you may be stuck in a meta-pattern: being "creative" in a predictable way. Signs:
- All your "creative" options are quirky one-word names
- You always default to the same emotional lens
- Your rule-breaking follows a pattern (always irreverent, always minimal, always retro)

Fix: pick a lens you've never used. Apply a constraint from a category you usually skip. The discomfort means it's working.
